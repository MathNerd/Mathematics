Theorem VII.29 (Bolzano-weierstrass) 1. Every bounded sequence has at least one subsequential finite limit. 2. Every unbounded above sequence has a subsequential limit of $+\infty$. 3. Every unbounded below sequence has a subsequential limit of $-\infty$. ---------- Proof First we’ll define the *midpoint function* $mid:\mathbb{R}\times\mathbb{R}\rightarrow\mathbb{R}$ as: $\forall a,b\in\mathbb{R}, mid(a,b):=\frac{a+b}{2}$ Now we’ll show that $\forall a,b\in\mathbb{R}, a\leq b\rightarrow a\leq mid(a,b)\leq b$: Let $a,b\in\mathbb{R}$ be such that $a\leq b$, Then $2a=a+a\leq a+b$, And thus $a\leq \frac{a+b}{2}=mid(a,b)$, Also $a+b\leq b+b=2b$, And thus $mid(a,b)=\frac{a+b}{2}\leq b$, By combining both inequalities we get $a\leq mid(a,b)\leq b$ as was to be shown. Now we’ll show that $\forall a,b\in\mathbb{R}, b-mid(a,b)=mid(a,b)-a=\frac{1}{2}(b-a)$: Let $a,b\in\mathbb{R}$, Then $b-mid(a,b)=b-\frac{a+b}{2}=\frac{2b-a-b}{2}=\frac{b-a}{2}$, Also $mid(a,b)-a=\frac{a+b}{2}-a=\frac{a+b-2a}{2}=\frac{b-a}{2}$, By combining those two equalities we get $b-mid(a,b)=mid(a,b)-a=\frac{1}{2}(b-a)$ as was to be shown. ---------- Let $\{a_n\in\mathbb{R}\}_{n=1}^\infty$ be a bounded sequence of real numbers, Then $\exists r,s\in\mathbb{R}, \forall n\in\mathbb{N},r\leq a_n\leq s$, And in particular we have $r\leq s$, We’ll show that $\exists t\in\mathbb{R},t\in\mathscr{L}\big(\{a_n\in\mathbb{R}\}_{n=1}^\infty\big)$: We’ll build by induction on $k\in\mathbb{N}$ two sequences $\{r_k\in\mathbb{R}\}_{k=1}^\infty$ and $\{s_k\in\mathbb{R}\}_{k=1}^\infty$ such that they will satisfy: 1. $\forall k\in\mathbb{N}, r_k\leq r_{k+1}$. 2. $\forall k\in\mathbb{N}, s_k\geq s_{k+1}$. 3. $\forall k\in\mathbb{N}, r_k\leq s_k$. 4. $\forall k\in\mathbb{N}, s_k-r_k=\frac{1}{2}(s-r)$. ---------- **For** $k=1$: Let $t_0:=mid(r,s)$, Since $r\leq s$, We conclude that $r\leq mid(r,s)\leq s$, And thus $r\leq t_0\leq s$, And we conclude that $[r,t_0]\cup [t_0,s]=[r,s]$, Now Since $\forall n\in\mathbb{N}, r\leq a_n\leq s$, We conclude that $\forall n\in\mathbb{N},a_n\in [r,s]$.
